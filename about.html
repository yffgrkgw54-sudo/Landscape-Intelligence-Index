Landscape Intelligence

A Networked Timeline of Planetary Epistemologies

Overview

This visualization maps landscape intelligence across 4.5 billion years—from the Earth's formation through Indigenous epistemologies, modern Earth system science, and contemporary critical theory. It reveals how different knowledge systems understand, model, and interact with planetary processes.

The timeline integrates three source datasets: the Landscape Intelligence Timeline (documenting forms of landscape knowing), the Jaco Challenge Citations (focused on rare earth elements and material flows), and Territorial Agency/Palmesino lectures (connecting architectural practice to planetary computation).

The Cyanometer Palette

The colour scheme draws from the Cyanometer, an 18th-century instrument invented by Horace-Bénédict de Saussure to measure the blueness of the sky. The nine thematic categories are mapped to this spectrum—from the deepest Prussian blue (Deep Time) to the palest sky-white (Ancient & Classical).

Indeterminacy Positions

A five-point scale assessing whether entries describe processes trending toward coherence (concentration, organisation) or dissipation (entropy, degradation):

Position Value Description
CLEAR-COHERENT 5 Unambiguous value—Indigenous knowledge systems, symbiotic evolution, functional recycling
SEARCH-PROMISING 4 Trending toward coherence—emerging recovery technologies, rights of nature
PHASE-TRANSITION 3 Genuinely undetermined—mass extinctions as system resets, paradigm shifts
SEARCH-DISSIPATING 2 Trending toward waste—species decline, knowledge erosion
CLEAR-DISSIPATION 1 Unambiguous entropy increase—extractive collapse, irreversible contamination

Five Criteria

Each entry is scored (1-5) across five dimensions:

Criterion Description
CLOSURE-MATERIAL Material cycle closure—how well the system closes material loops
METABOLIC-EFFICIENCY Energy/matter processing—efficiency of metabolic transformation
TIMESCALE-BRIDGING Temporal integration—capacity to bridge across time scales
PATTERN-PERSISTENCE Form maintenance—ability to maintain coherent patterns
SELF-RECOGNITION Systemic self-awareness—recognition of system's own operation

Using the Interface

Network View: Force-directed graph showing entries as nodes and connections as links. Nodes can be dragged; double-click to centre on a node. Toggle connection types and colour modes using the toolbar.

Radial View: Entries arranged in concentric rings by category, useful for seeing the distribution across thematic areas.

Timeline View: Chronological list grouped by category, with logarithmic temporal scaling to accommodate deep time alongside recent events.

Filtering: Use the sidebar filters to show/hide entries by category, source, indeterminacy position, or temporal phase. Search by title, description, or keywords.

Adding Entries: Click "+ Add Entry" to contribute new entries through the form. Entries are stored in browser session (not persisted to server).
